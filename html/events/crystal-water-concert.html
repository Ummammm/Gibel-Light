CRYSTAL WATER CONCERT

(Orris Prime)

Edele, the wanderer.

Once per orbit, when the sky above Orris Prime clears to a perfect sapphire hue and the stillwater lake beneath the crystal caverns settles into mirror-like calm, the planet holds its most beloved acoustic tradition: the Crystal Waters Concert. No amplification. No artificial resonance. Just voice, instrument, and stone, all aligned to perform in one of the most acoustically pure environments in the Gibelverse.

The concert is held in a naturally formed amphitheater that cups the edge of the lake. Its walls are carved from refractive crystal that bends sound the way glass bends light, letting each note echo not outward, but inward, wrapping the listener in a spiral of melody. As the audience settles onto softstone benches under the indigo sky, the lake begins to glow faintly, as if the water itself is anticipating the music.

Musicians come from across the system, bringing hollowtone violas, skyflutes, and deep-breath horns carved from old comet shell. The compositions are written not to impress, but to attune, slow, layered arrangements meant to sync with the water’s surface vibrations. Each time a note is struck, the lake ripples back, creating light patterns that dance across the cavern dome. You hear the music. But you see it too.

Between songs, the space is silent, not awkwardly so, but reverently. The lake listens. The crystals hold the quiet. Some listeners cry. Others close their eyes and just breathe in rhythm with the chords. The final piece is always performed solo, facing the water, allowing the player and the planet to finish the concert together. No words are spoken after. The audience rises and walks out in silence, their footsteps muffled by mossglass underfoot. The Crystal Waters Concert is not a spectacle. It’s a communion, between artist and atmosphere, listener and stillness, planet and presence. You don’t leave humming the melody. You leave remembering the feeling of being heard without ever having to speak.
