MEMORY WELL FESTIVAL

(Lunara)

Edele, the wanderer.

Held only during the dusk apex of Lunara’s longest bloomphase, the Memory Well Festival is a planetary invitation to pause, reflect, and descend gently into yourself. Scattered across the fields and gentle ridgelines of Lunara are its famed memory wells, shimmering, psionically active pools that reflect not just light, but feeling. During this sacred cycle, the wells begin to glow brighter, calling to travelers and locals alike to come sit beside them... and remember,

Participants are guided by soft-voiced Waykeepers, trained meditation guides who don’t tell you what to feel, but help you follow the emotional thread already tugging at your sleeve. Groups move from bloomfields into quiet basins where petals drift on water and the sky turns mauve and silver. Each person takes their turn stepping into the water, not to bathe, but to connect. The wells don’t show visions. They invite resonance, surfacing memories long-buried or half-healed, always gently, always at the pace you allow

Throughout the festival, reflection circles form around the wells. Some participants share what surfaced , a memory of laughter long forgotten, a moment of forgiveness, a name whispered one last time. Others stay silent, simply warmed by the soft hum of collective feeling. Around each well are small alcoves for journaling, sketching, or just breathing with your hands on the moss. Time slows. The planet listens

Each evening ends with a light-floating ritual, where attendees release small orbs into the air, not lanterns, but memory-glows: warm pulses of color imbued with the feeling they chose to let go or carry forward. The sky fills with quiet drifting light, and no two glows are the same. Some burn bright. Others barely flicker. All are valid. All are seen. The Memory Well Festival isn’t about healing quickly or loudly. It’s about permission. To feel. To sit. To cry softly under a lavender sky. To be witnessed, not by people, necessarily, but by a planet that gently says, “It’s okay to feel it now.”
