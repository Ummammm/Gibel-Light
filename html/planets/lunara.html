LUNARA

Pastel bloomfields & healing memory wells at dusk.

Edele, the wanderer.

Lunara is often referred to as the heart that still remembers, a planet where memory is not just preserved, but felt. Nestled among the gentler orbits of the Gibelverse, it glows faintly even from orbit, thanks to its vast fields of bioluminescent flora and psionically sensitive terrain. Travelers often describe their first step onto Lunara as a sensation of being "welcomed by something ancient and kind." The planet’s surface is covered in what are known as bloomfields, soft expanses of flora that shift hue and light in response to emotional presence. Anger might bring violet flashes; longing may turn petals silver; joy creates radiant gold blooms that ripple outward. These fields act as a living mirror, allowing visitors to see what they feel often before they even understand it themselves. No two paths through Lunara are ever the same, because the planet gently shifts with its visitors. At the heart of many bloomfields lie floating memory wells naturally occurring psionic pools suspended a few meters above ground. These shimmering basins allow those who approach them to surface forgotten memories, dreams left unspoken, or emotions buried beneath the day-to-day. The effect is never overwhelming; instead, it’s described as a gentle pull, like a song you once knew being hummed back to you. Many describe the experience as cathartic, even sacred. It's no surprise that Lunara has become a destination for healing pilgrimages, artists in search of emotional clarity, and anyone seeking to understand themselves a little more gently. Because of its deeply personal nature, Lunara is not a place for spectacle, it’s a planet of stillness, reflection, and slow awe. Sunset walks here are often silent, except for the occasional wind-chime flora or the soft hum of the memory wells themselves. If you come, come with a soft heart. The planet will remember you, and if you're ready, it might help you remember yourself. Some travelers leave Lunara with nothing but silence. Not because they found no answers, but because the questions finally stopped shouting. It’s said that if you return often enough, the bloomfields will begin to recognize you, responding not just to your presence, but to your path. There’s comfort in knowing a planet remembers your footsteps, not to trap you in the past, but to show you how far you’ve walked since.
