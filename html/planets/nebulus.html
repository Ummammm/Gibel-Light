NEBULUS

Cloud-city orbiting a nebula—skywalk cafés included.

Edele, the wanderer.

Nebulus isn’t on a planet, it orbits a nebula like a thought circling an idea. Suspended in deep space, this city-station curves around the edges of the Virid Bloom Nebula, wrapped in soft-glass panels and long, glowing walkways that make you feel like you're floating in slow motion. No matter where you are in the city, the nebula is always there, breathing color into the walls, blooming across the skyline like a cosmic daydream. Built as both an observatory and a sanctuary, Nebulus evolved into something far more intimate: a place of drifting conversations, late-night music, and gentle connections between wanderers. You won’t find bustling markets or tour hubs here, you’ll find skywalk cafés, libraries without shelves, and lounges that play jazz made entirely from ship signal harmonics. The atmosphere is hush-luxury: not elite, just quietly attuned to those who are tired of pretending they aren’t soft. There’s no “main attraction,” because the whole city is an ambient experience. Maybe you’ll sip dreamroot tea beside a synth bard who remembers your name from a past life. Maybe you’ll wander into a dome theater projecting the star-patterns of extinct worlds. Or maybe, and most likely, you’ll sit in silence while the nebula wraps its light around you, and realize that’s all you needed. At night (though time means little here), the city hums softly, not with noise, but with presence. Some travelers stay for weeks. Some never leave. Those who do often return to the same booth in the same café, finding their cup already waiting. Nebulus remembers gently. Not with data. With care. This place isn’t for the loud or the lost, it’s for the in-between. For those who need to orbit something beautiful for a while before re-entering the world. If you’ve ever needed to feel small in the best possible way… this city will hold you there, and never ask why.

Traveler Tips:
• Pack light — gravity here is fluid and changes slightly between decks.
• Skywalks are open-air: bring goggles or a hooded aura-wrap.
• Visit during Dustfall Tide (cycle 3), when stardust drifts form temporary glowing bridges.

Known Establishments:
• Cloud Ninefold : A weightless café that rotates as you sip, with 360º nebula views.
• The Shroudwell : A meditation dome where your thoughts are visualized in floating light strings.
