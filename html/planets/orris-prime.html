ORRIS PRIME

Sapphire skies and crystal caves that glow at twilight.

Edele, the wanderer.

Orris Prime is a planet carved from clarity, its skies are impossibly blue, its terrain dotted with sprawling crystal cave systems that hum with quiet resonance. From above, the planet appears as a sapphire orb wrapped in soft light, but its true wonder lies below the surface, where the land opens into vast crystalline caverns that glow gently in twilight. These aren’t tourist gems, they’re living acoustics, shaped over millennia to reflect both light and sound. Wanderers here speak in low voices, not out of reverence, but necessity, even the smallest sound can send shivers of light across the walls. Echo chambers, naturally formed, turn whispers into music and emotions into color. The deeper you walk, the more your presence becomes part of the landscape. Many say that Orris doesn’t just reflect, it receives. Your thoughts hum through the stone, and the planet listens back. Above ground, the lakes are still as glass, reflecting the sky with such clarity it’s often impossible to tell where earth ends and stars begin. Mirrorlake Orria, in particular, is said to hold “the memory of the sky.” During certain alignments, visitors gather here for the Crystal Waters Concert, where instruments are tuned to the resonance of the lake itself, and the music is visible, blooming in concentric ripples of light and sound. Orris Prime isn’t loud. It doesn’t demand attention. But if you sit in one of its caverns long enough, if you let your heartbeat slow and your breath fall quiet, you might just hear yourself for the first time. Those who return to Orris often say the planet changes with you, that the caverns reflect not just sound, but growth. A whisper you left years ago might greet you differently when you return. Not because it’s changed, but because you have. The echo doesn’t lie. It simply reminds you who you were… and offers space for who you’re becoming.

• The best views are from the edge of Cobalt Drift, where crystals meet cliffside wind tunnels.

Known Establishments:
• The Blueveil Pavilion : An open-air observatory with echo-chime gardens.
