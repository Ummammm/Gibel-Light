Watched the Crystal Sky Bloom
ceremony, brought tears.

– Edele

They tell you not to expect anything. “Sometimes it doesn’t happen,” the crystalwarden said as we entered the basin. “But if it does… you’ll feel it before you see it.” I didn’t quite know what that meant — until I did.

The amphitheater on Orris Prime was silent. Not heavy, not tense. Just quiet the way a cathedral is quiet, even without walls. We sat in a ring of stone-carved benches, half-submerged in the twilight lake, the water so still it reflected stars that hadn’t even arrived yet. Then, as the sky shifted from blue to deep violet, the first shimmer began.

It didn’t start big. Just a pulse. A flicker. And then, the sky bloomed. Crystals embedded in the cliff walls caught the resonance of the setting sun and scattered it upward into the open air. Refracted waves of light — pale gold, lavender, silver, and rose — formed slow spirals overhead, drifting like petals on the wind. They weren’t just beautiful. They were felt. Something deep inside me cracked open in the gentlest way, and I realized I had tears on my face. No one spoke. I don’t think they could.

The ceremony lasted exactly as long as it needed to. No countdown. No climax. Just waves of light rising and falling in the silence, as if the planet itself was exhaling. When it ended, the crystals dimmed, and the lake rippled once — as if saying, there, that’s enough for now. People left slowly. Reverently. No one asked questions. We just… carried it with us. I didn’t take a photo. I don’t want one. There are some moments that belong only to the version of you who sat there and let the sky sing. The you who was still, soft, and open enough to bloom with it.
