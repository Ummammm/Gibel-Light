An orchid-petal drone
serenaded me in Hedra.

– Edele

It started with music in the distance — soft, fluttering, almost curious. I thought it was someone playing a bloomharp in the floating garden. But when I turned the corner past a drifting trellis of featherstem, I saw it: a drone, hovering just above the orchid path, its casing shaped like a single open blossom. And it was… singing.

Not in any language I recognized — but in tone. In light. In movement. It let out a soft chord as I approached, and the petals on its chassis shimmered to a subtle pink, like it was blushing. At first, I thought it was part of the festival installation — some elegant piece of programming. But then it rotated, slowly, to face me… and released another note. Higher. Brighter. Almost as if it liked me.

The locals call them serenaders — semi-sentient drones that roam Hedra’s floating orchid gardens during bloomseason. Not programmed to entertain. Just to respond. To mirror. To reflect whatever beauty they sense around them — and sometimes, that includes you. Mine hovered gently in front of me as I walked. Played a melody that shifted with my breath. Danced slightly above the blossom arches like it was inviting me to follow.

I did. We walked for nearly an hour. Sometimes it led. Sometimes I did. No words passed between us — just tone and petal, shimmer and smile. At one point, it dipped close, and a small orchid unfurled from its base. It hovered long enough for me to take it, then rose in a spiral and disappeared into the canopy, song trailing behind like ribbon in the wind. I pressed the orchid between the pages of my journal that night. It's still there, dried and quiet. But sometimes, when I open to that page, I swear I hear that first note again — soft, bright, curious. Like it remembered me too.
