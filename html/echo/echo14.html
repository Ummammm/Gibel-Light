Chased pollen-lanterns
during Sapling Seedfall.

– Edele

They rise just before twilight — soft, glowing orbs made from bark-thread and breathlight, tied loosely to seedstrings and released into the air like promises. Pollen-lanterns. I’d seen them from afar before, drifting above the canopies of Auriel’s Rest like golden stars that had forgotten how to fall. But this time… I was close enough to chase them.

The forest clearing was filled with laughter — quiet, scattered, never overpowering. Elders tied lanterns with practiced hands while children darted between trees, arms stretched skyward. I didn’t expect to join them. I was just there to observe. But when one of the smaller lanterns dipped low in front of me, trailing its thread like an invitation, something in me — ancient and unexplainably joyful — ran.

We chased them through pollen haze and rootpaths, between swaying saplings and high-bough arches. The air shimmered with gold, the kind of gold that settles on your skin like a blessing. I laughed. Out loud. The kind of laugh that escapes before you have time to be self-conscious. A small child beside me pointed at a lantern just above our heads and yelled, “That one’s yours!” And it felt true.

Eventually, I caught one. Not by force — it landed softly in my palm like it had chosen me. Inside was a single glowing seed, gently humming in rhythm with my pulse. A nearby gatherer offered me a seedcatcher to keep it safe. I didn’t ask what kind of tree it would become. I think that’s part of the magic: you only know once you’ve planted it. Later that night, I tied my own lantern — with a wish written inside, folded into a slip of dreamleaf. I let it go with no ceremony, just a breath and a hope. And as it rose into the warm dusk alongside a hundred others, I knew: some parts of healing don’t require effort. Just light. And the courage to run toward it.
