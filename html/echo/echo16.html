Heard the sand
hum as a song in Eiros.

– Edele

At first, I thought it was the wind. Just the usual desert breeze, sweeping through the coral-pink dunes of Eiros like it always did. But then I stopped walking, closed my eyes, and realized: the sound wasn’t around me — it was underneath me. The sand was humming.

They call it song-sand, tuned naturally by the mineral composition and wind harmonics of the region. But that doesn’t do it justice. It’s not a trick of the terrain. It’s not background noise. It’s a voice — soft, low, and utterly intimate. The kind that feels like it’s not speaking to you, but with you. Responding.

I walked barefoot, slow, and the hum shifted with me — rising slightly when I stepped lighter, dipping into deeper tones when my breath grew heavy. It wasn’t music in the usual sense. No melody. No rhythm. Just tone that felt like emotion given shape. I sat on a high dune as the sun melted into the horizon and let the desert sing me still. I didn't want to capture it. I just wanted to be in it.

Locals told me afterward that Eiros isn’t a place for answers. It’s a place for honesty. That the sand will reflect whatever you bring into it — restlessness, longing, peace — and turn it into sound. It made sense. I'd come with a thousand questions I didn’t have the strength to ask. I left with none of them answered… but somehow felt okay anyway. Even now, sometimes, when I close my eyes at night and the world finally quiets, I swear I can feel the hum again — somewhere low, somewhere warm. A gentle reminder from a planet that doesn’t shout, but still knows your name.
