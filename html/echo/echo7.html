I tent-camped by a
biolava pond on Pyrrha.

– Edele

It was never supposed to be an overnight stay. I’d planned a quick stop — just a peek at the lava flows before moving on to the skybridges at dawn. But Pyrrha had other plans. The guide simply pointed toward the path with a smile and said, “You’ll know when to stop.” I didn’t know what that meant — until I saw the pond. Tucked between two quiet ridges, the biolava pond glowed like a sleeping sun. It didn’t bubble or burn. It pulsed — low, rhythmic, gentle. The warmth reached me before the light did. Not hot, but comforting, like the heat of a blanket left out in morning sun. I set up camp at the edge of the stone shelf, my tent illuminated from below like an ember in a dream. And then I sat. I didn’t even take my boots off for the first hour. Just stared. Let it wash over me.

Later that night, I opened the flap and lay on my back, feet pointed toward the pond. The stars above twinkled. The lava below glowed. For a strange moment, it felt like I was suspended between two skies — one deep and cold, one warm and alive. In the middle, there I was. Breathing. Healing. Not thinking too hard about it for once.

The lava never spoke. But it felt like it did. Like it was whispering through the stone beneath me: “You're not a problem to solve. You’re a body that needs rest.” And under that golden light, I believed it. I slept deeper than I had in weeks. No dreams. Just glow.

In the morning, the pond had cooled slightly, its pulse slower. I whispered a thank you into the steam and packed my things. As I walked away, I looked back once. The pond glowed brighter for just a second — like it was smiling.
