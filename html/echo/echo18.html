I found peace in a
Moss & Ember fire circle.

– Edele

I don’t remember when I sat down. Only that I exhaled for the first time in what felt like weeks.

The moss beneath me was warm from the nearby rooffire, and the glow from the emberpit made everything feel hushed. Like even the trees were listening. Around me, strangers sat shoulder to shoulder, wrapped in loose blankets and shadows, stirring soup with slow spoons or simply staring into the flames. No introductions. No explanations. Just the quiet understanding that this space didn’t need either.

The drums had stopped hours before, replaced by the soft creak of wind in the canopy and the occasional pluck of a moss-harp. Someone passed me a bowl of glow-mushroom stew. I hummed faintly when I held it — not from heat, but from memory. I didn’t ask who made it. I just took a bite, and for some reason, that bite felt like an apology I didn’t know I needed.

At some point, someone laughed — the kind that starts low and ends in tears. No one asked why. Someone else whispered a story about a lost brother and left a carved stone by the fire. I pressed my fingers into the moss and felt it pulse back — not strongly, just enough to say, “you’re here,” and that’s enough. And it was. When I finally stood to leave, the fire pulsed once more. I touched the ground lightly with my palm in thanks. I didn’t take anything with me... except the stillness. It’s followed me ever since.
