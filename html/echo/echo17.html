Practiced wind-harvest
crafts in Zephyra’s fair.

– Edele

You don’t just visit the Wind-Harvest Fair on Zephyra — you become part of it. The moment I stepped onto the floating platform, the air felt alive around me, tugging gently at my sleeves like it was asking, “Wanna play?” Breezeboards floated in slow clusters, anchored by drift-chimes and skyfruit stalls. Everywhere I looked: color, motion, invention. I wandered past a petal-glider booth, a wind-carver’s demonstration, and a snack cart selling softleaf puffs that dissolved into cool mist on your tongue. But it was the kite workshop that stopped me — not the kites themselves, but the laughter. The real kind.

The artisan waved me in without a word. Their hands moved with the ease of someone who'd done this their whole life — weaving sunthread through breeze-cloth, attaching tail-ribbons scented with sky-lavender. “The wind knows your mood,” they said. “Let it choose the shape.” I didn’t know what that meant… until I tied the last loop, tossed it into the air, and watched my kite curve upward into a spiral I hadn’t planned.

It didn’t just fly — it danced. Twisting, pausing, dipping, rising. A reflection, maybe, of something in me that needed air. Around me, others released their own kites, each moving differently: some direct, some chaotic, some so gentle they barely floated. It wasn’t about performance. It was about release. Letting something go, and letting the sky catch it.

When the fair started drifting toward its next wind current, I folded my kite carefully and tied a sprig of windheather to its frame. I didn’t take it home. I gave it to a passing child who said hers had fallen into a cloud. She smiled, I nodded, and the platform carried us in opposite directions. And just like that — the wind had taught me something.
