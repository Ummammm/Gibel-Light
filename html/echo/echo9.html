Got lost in a
Cloudfall Day water-maze.

– Edele

It was supposed to be a quick stroll. Just a light step into the maze, trace the soft-stone paths, maybe catch a few mist-sculptures before sunset. But Cloudfall Day has its own plans. And Sylphae? Sylphae loves to rearrange joy. The maze was built on shifting platforms — soft coralstone tiles floating gently on the surface of the overflow canals. As I stepped in, the mist thickened, blurring the lines between water, path, and sky. At first, it was enchanting

bridges curved like ribbons, lanterns hung from bending reeds, and music drifted in from who-knows-where. I followed the laughter of children ahead… until it turned into echoes. Until I realized I hadn’t seen another person in half an hour. Was I worried? Not exactly. Lost? Very. But the mist was warm. The breeze gentle. I started noticing details I wouldn’t have otherwise — flowers growing from the canal walls, blooming in time with my footsteps. A flute note that repeated every time I turned left. A soft voice humming a tune I swear I’d never heard, but somehow knew. It didn’t feel like being trapped. It felt like being… invited deeper.

I eventually sat at the center of a spiral bridge, feet in the water, watching soft droplets rise and fall in gravity-play patterns. That’s where a local boy found me — maybe ten, maybe timeless — and offered me a glowfruit jelly from a pouch. I asked how he found his way through. He shrugged and said, “I don’t try to leave. The maze lets you out when it’s done with you.” He was right.

Twenty minutes later, the mist parted. The canal reconnected. And I walked out the other side like nothing had happened — except something had. I’d been still. I’d wandered without worry. And somewhere in those foggy steps, the maze hadn’t just lost me… it had helped me find a little more of myself.
