Tasted moon-beam tea in
Crescenta’s midnight cafe.

– Edele

The thing about Crescenta is that it’s always twilight — the kind that makes you forget how long you’ve been awake, or if you were ever tired to begin with. I found the cafe by accident, nestled between two curved stone alleys lit with soft biolume lanterns that flickered like they were in on a joke I hadn’t heard yet. The sign just read: Stillwarm. And inside, everything felt like a pause.

Low tables carved from lunar driftwood. Petal-woven mats. Soft music that sounded like lullabies humming themselves to sleep. The server didn’t hand me a menu. They simply asked, “What kind of quiet do you need tonight?” And I, without thinking, replied, “The kind that remembers.” They nodded once, disappeared behind a curtain, and returned with a small porcelain cup glowing faintly from within.

Moon-beam tea. I’d heard of it — steeped under specific light conditions, charged with psionic frequencies, flavored with the memory of something you didn’t know you missed. I lifted the cup, and it shimmered softly — pale blue and starlight white, like holding a piece of night sky in my hands. The first sip hit slow. Sweet, earthy, with a finish like distant laughter.

And then I felt it — not a memory, but the mood of one. My body curled up in a chair I no longer owned. The sound of a clock ticking in another room. Someone brushing my hair, once, long ago. No images. Just feeling. Like the tea had dipped into the well of my inner quiet and pulled up the exact fragment I needed most. I stayed until the lanterns dimmed and the shop gently folded itself closed. The server didn’t say goodbye — just set a small starflower on my saucer and let me leave when I was ready. I still don’t know if that cafe exists all the time… or only when someone needs to remember something soft. But I think about that tea often. And sometimes, when I close my eyes, I taste it again.
