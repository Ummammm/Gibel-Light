Caught an aurora reflection in
a crystal cave on Orris Prime

– Edele

Wasn’t planning to go deep into the caves. I’d stopped just outside the Velarian archway, content to sip my morning brew and watch the tourists echo their way into the glittering dark. But then the light shifted — not around me, but within the cave, and something quiet in me said, go now.

I walked alone. The further I went, the more the world faded — until all I could hear were my own footsteps and the occasional ping of a distant water droplet. The cave glowed in gradients of sapphire and soft rose, and the crystals around me responded faintly with light, humming in low tones like a choir that had learned how to rest. It was calming in a way I didn’t expect — not silencing, but settling.

And then, I turned a corner… and saw it. The ceiling opened into a vast dome of crystal, mirrored just right to catch the sky through a narrow crevice above. And there it was — the aurora, dancing across the opening like silk on the wind, reflected in every facet of the cavern around me. I couldn’t tell where the sky ended and the cave began. It was everywhere, wrapping around me in greens, golds, and silent blues. I stood still for so long I forgot I had a body. Forgot I’d ever been anything but this: a speck of light, held gently in something greater.

I didn’t take a picture. I didn’t want to. I just sat there, hand against the cool crystal floor, and let the moment echo through me. And when I finally stood to leave, I noticed my shadow dancing along the walls — refracted by hundreds of points of light. Like even after I was gone, a piece of me would keep dancing there.
