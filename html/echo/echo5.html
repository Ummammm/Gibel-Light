I whispered with the
Arboris trees at dawn.

– Edele

I’d taken the highroot path before sunrise, following the faint glow of pulsevine lanterns strung between branches like sleepy stars. The forest was quiet, not with silence, but with listening. You could feel it in the moss — the way it held your steps like it knew where you were going before you did. I wasn’t headed anywhere special. I just needed… stillness. Arboris offers that in abundance.

At the first light of dawn, I reached the Whisper Circle — a grove of ancient goldenwood trees whose trunks bend inward, forming a soft, sheltering ring around a moss-cradled clearing. The air felt charged. Not with energy. With presence. Like something was waiting. So I sat. Placed my palms on the earth. And let the words come.

I whispered things I hadn’t said in years — worries I’d buried, hopes I didn’t know I still carried. Nothing loud. Just breath and truth. And slowly, the trees responded. Not in speech, but in resonance. The moss beneath me pulsed faintly. The leaves above stirred with no wind. The bark shimmered in lines of soft gold, moving with my heartbeat. It didn’t feel like magic. It felt like being heard by something old enough to do it well.

Before I left, I pressed my forehead to the bark of the oldest tree in the circle. It was warm. Alive. And just for a second, I felt the pulse again — not beneath my hands, but inside my chest. A rhythm that wasn’t mine… but fit perfectly. I walked back in silence, lighter than I’d arrived. The trees didn’t say goodbye. They didn’t need to.
