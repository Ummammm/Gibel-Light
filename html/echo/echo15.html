Drifted above Vesper Isles
on a biocloud gondola.

– Edele

They told me not to speak for the first five minutes. “Let the air introduce itself,” the gondolier whispered, before guiding me up into the soft, glowing layers above the Vesper Isles. I thought it was poetic flair — until we lifted off, and the silence became something sacred.

The biocloud gondola moved like it wasn’t in a hurry. Pillowed with breathable fog and lined with pulsevine cushions, it glided effortlessly across the sky paths between floating isles. Below us, the golden mists shifted with the wind like a slow-motion tide. I could see distant treetop gardens flickering through the haze, bridges coiling into the clouds, and smaller gondolas trailing like drifting thoughts.

Time didn’t pass normally up there. The sun filtered through the vapor in long, soft rays, catching on dew and dust until the entire sky looked painted. The gondolier never spoke unless I asked something — and I didn’t ask much. Instead, I leaned against the side rail, fingers brushing through the cloudtrail, watching shapes form and dissolve in silence. It felt less like sightseeing, more like dreaming with your eyes open.

Halfway through, he handed me a warm drink made from mistroot and skycane. No name. No explanation. Just warmth and a sense of… arrival. I think that’s when I started crying. Not from sadness — from relief. There’s a kind of release that only comes when you realize the world doesn’t need anything from you in return. The gondola kept drifting. And I let go of things I didn’t even realize I’d been holding. When we descended back into the golden mist and touched down at a small moss dock, I didn’t say thank you. I just looked at the gondolier, and he nodded. No transaction. Just grace in motion. I still dream about that ride — not as memory, but as a place I know I can return to whenever the world feels too heavy.
