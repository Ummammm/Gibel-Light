Wrote a letter inside a
memory-well and left it in Lunara.

– Edele

The guide didn’t walk me all the way in. She just pointed toward the well tucked beneath the bloomshade and said, “Write what your heart still carries. The water will know what to do.” I nodded like I understood — but I didn’t. Not really. You can’t understand something like Lunara’s memory-wells until you’re sitting at the edge, knees tucked in, watching your reflection flicker like it remembers you better than you remember yourself.

The writing desk was carved from moonwood, soft to the touch and slightly warm. A stack of petal-paged stationery rested beside a glass stylus that shimmered faintly when I picked it up. I sat there for a long time, stylus hovering, heart thudding, unsure what I was supposed to say. What do you write when no one’s going to read it… except maybe the planet?

Eventually, the words came. Not as poetry. Not as closure. Just… truth. Honest, messy, vulnerable. I wrote about the person I never got to say goodbye to. About the version of myself I left behind. About the guilt I carry on quiet days. The gratitude I never voiced. It wasn’t perfect. But it was real. When I finished, I folded the letter along the petal-creases and gently slid it into the water.

The well accepted it without a ripple. The glow rose around the note, turning it to light — no flame, no ash, just gentle shimmer that sank slowly until it was gone. I didn’t feel healed, exactly. But I felt lighter. Like I’d handed something off to be remembered on my behalf. I sat there until dusk. No more words. No more writing. Just the sound of petals drifting onto the surface of the water, and a soft breeze that felt like a thank-you I didn’t expect. I walked away empty-handed — and somehow, more whole than I’d been in years.
